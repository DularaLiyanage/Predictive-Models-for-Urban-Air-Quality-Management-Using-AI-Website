About Us

Meet the four researchers and two supervisors behind this IEEE ISDFS 2026 publication.

Our Research Group

We are a four-member final-year undergraduate research team from the Faculty of Computing, Sri Lanka Institute of Information Technology (SLIIT), supervised by Dinuka Wijendra and Thamali Dassanayake. Our work was published at the IEEE 14th International Symposium on Digital Forensics and Security (ISDFS) 2026 under DOI 10.1109/ISDFS69419.2026.11459026.

Research Team

Four undergraduate researchers, each leading a core component of the integrated AI system.

👨‍💻

Dulara Liyanage

AI / Hybrid Forecasting

Led the development of the hybrid SARIMAX–Temporal Fusion Transformer model for multi-pollutant forecasting. Responsible for feature engineering, Seasonality-Aware Imputation, and XAI attention analysis.

📧 dularaliyanage1651@gmail.com
SARIMAX-TFT XAI MICE
👩‍💻

Nimasha Vithanage

Spatial Estimation

Developed the ensemble spatial estimation framework for generating 1 km × 1 km pollution maps. Integrated geospatial datasets (SRTM, ESA WorldCover, WorldPop, OSM) and built the Flutter heatmap visualisation.

📧 nimashavithanage6@gmail.com
LSTM GPR / RBF Flutter
👩‍💻

Imasha Wijewardane

AQI Risk Alerting

Built the CEEMDAN–Seq2Seq-Attention pipeline for 24-hour AQI forecasting and automated health alert triggering. Integrated HYSPLIT transboundary risk indices, traffic scores, and CEA AQI breakpoints.

📧 imashawijewardane@gmail.com
CEEMDAN Seq2Seq HYSPLIT
👩‍💻

Nimasha Fernando

Anomaly Detection

Designed the GRU-based ensemble anomaly detection and event characterisation module. Implemented K-Means clustering for event categorisation and XGBoost + SHAP for causal driver analysis and preventive health tips.

📧 nimashavithanage6@gmail.com
GRU K-Means SHAP
