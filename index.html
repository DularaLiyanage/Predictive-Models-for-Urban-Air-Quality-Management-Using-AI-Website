About the Project

A brief overview of what we are building and why it matters.

This project focuses on developing an AI-powered system to monitor and forecast urban air quality in Sri Lanka using IoT sensors and advanced machine learning models.

Rapid urbanisation in cities like Colombo, Kandy, and Galle has led to a significant rise in air pollutants such as PM2.5, PM10, CO₂, and NO₂. Existing monitoring infrastructure is sparse, reactive, and unable to deliver actionable forecasts to citizens or policymakers.

Our solution integrates low-cost IoT sensor networks with LSTM and XGBoost forecasting models, a FastAPI back-end, and a cross-platform Flutter dashboard — giving communities real-time air quality data and 24-hour ahead forecasts.

Key Features

Four pillars that make this system comprehensive and impactful.

📡

Real-Time Monitoring

Low-cost IoT sensor nodes deployed across urban areas stream PM2.5, PM10, CO₂, NO₂, temperature and humidity data continuously via MQTT.

🤖

AI-Based Forecasting

LSTM and XGBoost models trained on historical and real-time data deliver accurate 24-hour ahead air quality index (AQI) predictions.

🔔

Smart Alerts System

Automated threshold-based and ML-triggered push notifications warn users of hazardous conditions before they escalate.

📱

Mobile Dashboard

A cross-platform Flutter app visualises AQI maps, sensor readings, forecasts, and personalised health advisories for citizens.
