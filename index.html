About the Project

A brief overview of what we built and why it matters for Sri Lanka.

Air pollution threatens public health in data-scarce urban areas like Sri Lanka, where the Central Environmental Authority (CEA) operates just two fixed automated monitoring stations, leaving cities like Colombo, Kandy, and Kurunegala without adequate coverage or forward-looking forecasts.

This research proposes an integrated AI framework with four modules: a hybrid SARIMAX–Temporal Fusion Transformer for multi-pollutant hourly forecasting; an ensemble spatial estimator (RBF, GP, KNN, IDW) for gap-filling pollution maps; a CEEMDAN–Seq2Seq-Attention pipeline for 24-hour AQI risk alerting; and a GRU-based anomaly detector with XGBoost + SHAP Explainable AI for causal transparency.

Validated on CEA historical data (2019–2024) from Battaramulla and Kandy stations, the framework outperforms baselines by 15–81% and delivers a real-time Flutter web dashboard aggregated via a FastAPI back-end.

Key Contributions

Four integrated AI modules enabling end-to-end proactive air quality management.

🔮

Hybrid Multi-Pollutant Forecasting

SARIMAX–TFT model captures both linear and non-linear volatilities for PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, and O₃. Achieves 81.6% RMSE reduction for ozone over baseline SARIMAX.

🗺️

Ensemble Spatial Estimation

Weighted RBF (0.25), GP (0.25), KNN (0.20), IDW (0.30) ensemble generates 1 km × 1 km pollution maps for unmonitored regions, visualised on a Flutter heatmap.

🚨

24-Hour AQI Risk Alerting

CEEMDAN decomposes non-stationary AQI signals; Seq2Seq-Attention forecasts 24-hour risk levels with R² = 0.9077, triggering automated health alerts via the mobile app.

🔍

Anomaly Detection + XAI

GRU ensemble achieves F1 = 0.79 for anomaly classification. XGBoost + SHAP explains causal drivers, outputting specific preventive public health tips in the mobile dashboard.
